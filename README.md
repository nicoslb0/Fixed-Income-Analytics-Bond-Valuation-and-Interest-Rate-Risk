# Fixed-Income-Analytics-Bond-Valuation-and-Interest-Rate-Risk

A quantitative investment research report evaluating a set of fixed income instruments, combining bond valuation, interest rate risk analysis, and Monte Carlo simulation to produce a data-driven investment recommendation.

Overview
This project, developed as part of a graduate Financial Data Analytics group assignment, positions the analysis within the context of a quantitative research team at an investment management firm exploring new fixed income investment strategies. Approximately five fixed income instruments are selected and analyzed using the valuation and risk frameworks covered in the course, culminating in a coherent investment research report supported by quantitative evidence.

Objective
The goal is to evaluate the valuation and risk characteristics of the selected bonds and assess their attractiveness as investment opportunities under varying interest rate conditions, ultimately delivering a clear, evidence-based investment recommendation.

Methodology
Instrument Selection
Approximately five fixed income instruments are selected to represent a manageable, diverse set of potential investment opportunities.

Valuation and Risk Analysis
Standard bond analytics are applied to assess pricing and risk characteristics, including yield-to-maturity, duration, and convexity measures, providing a framework for understanding each instrument's sensitivity to interest rate movements.

Monte Carlo Simulation
Simulation-based techniques are used to model how bond prices behave under alternative interest rate scenarios, allowing for a probabilistic assessment of valuation outcomes rather than relying on a single deterministic forecast.

Interest Rate Risk Assessment
The results of the valuation and simulation analysis are used to discuss the implications for interest rate risk exposure across the selected instruments, highlighting differences in sensitivity and stability.

Investment Recommendation
Findings are synthesized into a clear, quantitatively supported recommendation regarding the attractiveness of the analyzed bonds as investment opportunities.

Tools & Libraries
Language: R

Core techniques: bond pricing and yield analytics, duration/convexity risk measures, Monte Carlo simulation of interest rate scenarios
